You can travel to Cape Cod by car, bus, train, or plane


Car

You could drive there from NYC by taking the I-95, the New England turnpike, north through Connecticut, to Providence,Rhode Island. Then follow MA Route 25 onto the Cape. Once you are on the Cape, you can take Route 6 to the town of your choice and to the Upper Cape towns of Eastham, Wellfleet, and Provincetown. Scenic Route 6A travels along the northern shore of the lower cape, passing through the towns of Sandwich, East Sandwich, Barnstable, Dennis, and Brewster. MA Route 28 winds along the southern shore of the lower cape,passing through Bourne, East Falmouth, Hyannis, Harwich and Chatham to Orleans.


Bus

Traveling by bus is my favorite way to go since I don't drive. You can take the Peter Pan Bus from the Port Authority in NYC to Hyannis, Massachussettes. The bus ride is about 6 hours from NYC.


Train

Amtrak trains from New York's Pennsylvania Station go to the Providence Amtrak Station. Then you can take a taxi or city bus to the Providence Peter Pan Bus Terminal for the bus to Hyannis MA on Cape Cod.


Plane

JetBlue Airways has nonstop flights from New York to Barnstable Municipal Airport in Hyannis for $170 round-trip mid-week off-season, about $330 to $374 round-trip in the summer, up to $558+ for weekend flights. The flight takes just over one hour. Other flights, via Nantucket , may be more expensive, may take 3 hours or more, or even require an overnight stop. American Airlines, Delta Air Lines and United Airlines also have nonstop or one-stop flights.
